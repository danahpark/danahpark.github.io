I am a fourth-year PhD Candidate in the Department of Mechanical Engineering at Stanford University, where I am supported by the Stanford Graduate Fellowship and Kwanjeong Scholarship. At Stanford, I work with Prof. Ali Mani on computational fluid dynamics (CFD) research, modeling complex flow systems such as separated wall-bounded turbulent flows and investigating the physics therein. At the same time, I also work with Prof. Adrian Lozano Duran from Massachusetts Institute of Technology, trying to understand the underlying structures and energy cascades in turbulent flows using transformative tools.

I graduated from Seoul National University, Class of 2017, with a B.S.E. in Naval Architecture and Ocean Engineering, as the top student within College of Engineering. During my undergraduate times, I worked with Prof. Shin Hyung Rhee on multi-phase flows and CFD particularly for naval applications.

I am currently looking for an internship opportunity in the field including but not limited to CFD, applied computational physics, machine-learning, and high-performance scientific computing. Please feel free to contact me via email, and also check out my research.
